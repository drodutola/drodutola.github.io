🤖 Live Sample Question Thyroid · Medium Difficulty

A 34-year-old woman presents with palpitations, weight loss, and heat intolerance for 3 months. TSH is undetectable, free T4 is elevated. She is 10 weeks pregnant. Which of the following is the most appropriate initial management?

A Radioactive iodine ablation
B Total thyroidectomy
C Propylthiouracil (PTU) at lowest effective dose
D Methimazole 30mg daily
✓ Correct — C: PTU in first trimester

In pregnant patients with hyperthyroidism, RAI is absolutely contraindicated. PTU is preferred over methimazole in the first trimester due to methimazole's association with aplasia cutis and choanal atresia. Use the lowest effective dose; switch to methimazole after T1 due to PTU's hepatotoxicity risk. Surgery is reserved for cases refractory to medical therapy.
